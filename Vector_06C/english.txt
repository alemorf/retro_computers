Vector 06C

Year: 1987  
RAM: 64-320 KB  
ROM: 0.5 KB  
Processor: K580VM80A, 3 MHz  
Graphics: 512x256x4, 256x256x16, 256-color palette  
Sound: K580VI53 x3  
Group: Vector 06C  

The Vector 06C is a Soviet home computer with the most beautiful graphics. Its
considered the most beautiful because, at a resolution of 256x256, it can
display 16 true colors from a 256-color palette. Few computers of that era could
render pink for skin tones or brown for a tree trunk.

Upon powering on, the screen displays colored bars, making the Vector easily
recognizable among other computers. These bars show the computer's memory status
and indicate readiness to load a program from a cassette tape. As the program
loads, the bars fill in. Until the program is fully loaded, the computer cannot
perform other tasks. The 512-byte ROM contains only the cassette tape
bootloader. However, even after successfully loading a program, a secret key
combination is needed to start it.

The Vector's cassette recording method is more advanced than that of most other
8-bit computers. Programs are recorded in 256-byte blocks, each protected by a
checksum. If an error occurs during loading, only the faulty block needs to be
reloaded.

The computer can be upgraded to load from other storage devices by installing a
2 KB ROM containing bootloaders for cassette tapes, floppy disks, quasi-disks,
or external ROM. External ROM connects to the computers parallel port and can
store a favorite game, BASIC, or other programs.

The Vector has 64 KB of RAM. The ROM is disconnected from the address space and
does not interfere. Of the 64 KB, 8/16/24/32 KB are used for video memory in
256x256 modes with 2/4/8/16 colors, leaving 32 to 56 KB for programs. Each 8 KB
of memory represents a separate bit plane, enabling fast overlay of images on
backgrounds. Additional graphics modes include 512x256 with 2/4 colors and
hardware vertical screen scrolling. Hardware scrolling significantly speeds up
text-based programs and can be used in some games.

For sound output, three channels of the KR580VI53 timer chip are used, allowing
three-voice polyphony without heavily taxing the CPU, or digital sound (PWM
modulation) at 100% CPU load.

The KR580VM80A processor runs at an overclocked 3 MHz, though its documented
maximum is 2.5 MHz. However, the Vectors performance is not greater than the
Orion 128 with a 2.5 MHz processor because the Vectors processor is slowed by
the video controller. Instruction execution time is rounded up to four cycles.

The Vector uses a capacitive or reed switch keyboard, considered more convenient
and reliable, though the Vectors keyboard is slightly above average in
reliability and usability.

Expansions for the Vector included increasing RAM to 320 KB, adding a floppy
disk controller, integrating an AY-3-8910 sound synthesizer, and even replacing
the processor with a Z80.
